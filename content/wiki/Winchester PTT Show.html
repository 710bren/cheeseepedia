The show consisted of the main characters and moving props placed along the bannisters of the showroom, with the }}">Warblettes hung below the ceiling.

History

All characters were produced by }}">Fantasy Forest, mainly by its owner Harold Goldbrandsen. Fantasy Forest was previously a mascot costume manufacturer, making the Winchester show their first animatronic products created. Atari's Cyan Engineering (also known as the Grass Valley Team) would handle the creation of the }}">Winchester Control System, headed by Larry Emmons and Gary Waters.1

Animatronics

The }}">Chuck E. Cheese Proof of Concept Prototype was initially built as a concept piece for the location, but was never intended to be used inside the building. The main animatronics and their locations were:
  • }}">Winchester Chuck E. Cheese V1 (South West Corner)
  • }}">Winchester Crusty the Cat V1 (South East Corner)
  • }}">Winchester Pasqually (North Bannister)
  • }}">Winchester Jasper T. Jowls (East Bannister)
  • }}">Winchester Warblettes (Hung below ceiling in the center)
  • }}">Winchester Pizza Time Orchestra (Along East and South Bannisters)
  • }}">Winchester Flag Wavers (Along all Bannisters)
  • }}">Winchester Applause Board (West Bannister)
Both Chuck E. and Crusty would receive upgraded designs with more movements in August of 1977.
